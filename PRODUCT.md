# 5 Whys Career Studio

## Register

Brand (marketing/landing) + Product (interactive tools)

## Target Users

- Job seekers in transition (layoffs, career pivots, early-career)
- Professionals preparing for interviews or networking events
- People who prefer structured reflection over generic career advice
- Privacy-conscious users who refuse sign-up flows

## Product Purpose

A small studio of focused, interactive career tools that help users:

1. Articulate their career motivations (Five Whys)
2. Improve resume bullets with scoring and rewrite guidance (Resume Game)
3. Rehearse networking conversations with timers and feedback (Networking Practice)
4. Decode job descriptions and build interview packets (Interview Glow Up)

All tools are privacy-first: data stored locally, no accounts, no tracking.

## Brand Personality

- Calm and intentional (not urgent or salesy)
- Premium but approachable (not corporate, not playful)
- Trustworthy through transparency (open source, privacy-first)
- Crafted with care (attention to typography, spacing, motion)

## Tone

- Direct and honest, not motivational fluff
- Respectful of the user's intelligence
- Practical over inspirational
- Every word earns its place

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS landing pages with big-number hero metrics
- Purple-gradient-on-white AI slop
- Heavy glassmorphism cards
- Cookie-cutter shadcn/ui defaults without customization
- Over-animated page loads that delay content

## Strategic Design Principles

1. The tool should disappear into the task
2. Privacy is a feature, not a limitation — design for it
3. Typography and spacing are the primary visual hierarchy
4. Motion conveys state, never decoration
5. Every page should feel like it was crafted, not generated
