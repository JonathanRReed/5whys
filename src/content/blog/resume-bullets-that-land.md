Recruiters spend an average of eight seconds on a first-pass resume review. In that window, your bullets either signal competence or get skimmed. The difference is usually not the work you did—it is how you described it.

## The four-part formula

Every strong resume bullet names four things:

1. **The action** — what you did, using a strong verb.
2. **The scope** — the system, team, or audience involved.
3. **The method** — how you did it, with enough specificity to sound real.
4. **The result** — a number, percentage, or concrete outcome.

## Before and after

**Before:**  
*Responsible for improving onboarding process.*

**After:**  
*Redesigned the engineer onboarding playbook, cutting time-to-first-commit from 14 days to 4 days across a 30-person hiring wave.*

The before version is a responsibility. The after version is evidence. Recruiters scan for evidence.

## Weak verb replacements

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Responsible for | Led, Drove, Owned |
| Helped with | Partnered on, Co-designed |
| Worked on | Built, Launched, Shipped |
| Managed | Directed, Scaled, Grew |

## When you do not have numbers

Not every role produces metrics. When numbers are missing, use concrete scope:

- The team size affected.
- The process improved.
- The decision made under ambiguity.
- The risk reduced or compliance maintained.

Specificity beats inflation. A bullet that names the exact system you worked on reads stronger than one with vague percentage claims.
